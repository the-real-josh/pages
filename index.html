Toothless (May 2024 - May 2025)

  • My scratch-built level 1 high-power rocket.
  • Named after Toothless from How to Train Your Dragon because of its matte black appearance.
  • Experimented with various manufacturing techniques, including forged composites, over-wrapped plastic composites, vacuum bagging composites, 3D printed tube mandrels, and manual lathe.

  • Earned me the certification on its third launch attempt, reaching an apogee of about 4000 ft.

Morpheus (August 2024 - May 2025)

  • Built with SRT (A&M Sounding Rocketry Team) to fly in the 2025 IREC (International Rocket Engineering Competition).
  • I Built most of the aft section, rebuilt the nonstructural payload fairing, sanded and painted the vehicle.
  • Vehicle failed during its final 30,000 ft shot.
  • Failure was due to a lack of stiffness in the structural payload section.
  • I learned the importance considering all possible structural failure modes - no matter how "overbuilt" the structure might seem at a glance!

Sol Invictus (August 2025 - May 2026)

  • Student-designed hybrid rocket designed to compete in the 30K hybrids category for the 2026 IREC (International Rocket Engineering Competition).
  • Designed the nosecone
    • Selected the manufacturing process to fabricate an optimally strong, smooth nosecone with minimal effort.
    • Modeled the geometry in Ansys ACP Pre.
    • Ran Ansys Explicit Dynamics and Ansys Static Structural simulations to model ground impact.
  • Designed the fincan
    • Applied knowledge of how minimum diameter sounding rocket fincans are designed.
    • Modeled the composite fincan in Ansys ACP Pre
    • Simulated the deflection of the fins and fincan under load to verify adequate strength.
  • Designed the payload
    • Created a design that optimizes for minimal assembly and disassembly time.
    • Communicated with payload electronics people to make sure their needs were met.

StrideGuide (January 2023 - May 2023)

  • Designed and constructed a system to collect data on the user's running form through a network of accelerometers in order to provide feedback and reduce injury.
  • Built with Aggies Create.
  • Used Arduino RP2040 Connect main board and LIS3DH accelerometers. Used HTTP to send the data over the campus-wide WiFi.
  • Collected the data with a Python HTTPServer.
  • Led a team of six, dividing work into structures, data processing, electronics, and research. I put myself into the electronics team.
